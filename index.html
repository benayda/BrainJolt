Welcome to BrainJolt

Applying Howard Gardnerâ€™s Theory of Multiple Intelligences, my focus has been to reach a wide range of adult learners by adapting the presentation of educational content to people with different strongest intelligences. Therefore, for my PhD dissertation, which I completed in 2002, I developed a prototype adaptive online learning system and performed a formative evaluation of the prototype. I tested the prototype with a wide range of adult learners, gathering feedback for how it could be improved so that online learning can offer "anyone, anyhow" learning in addition to offering "anytime, anywhere" access. My entire dissertation is available online: Applying Multi-Intelligent Adaptive Hypermedia to Online Learning , and many other educational technologists have cited and built on my thesis research.

After I completed my PhD, I presented a summary of my dissertation at the Association for the Advancement of Computers in Education (AACE)annual E-Learn conference in Montreal, Canada in 2002, and my paper: Applying Multi-Intelligent Adaptive Hypermedia to Online Learning, was published in the conference proceedings.

I conducted further research and published papers on helping learners with different strongest intelligences.
